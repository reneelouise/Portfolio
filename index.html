Biography

Ambassador Peter Onu was a son of Africa, diplomat, civil servant, special advisor to the Nigerian government and philanthropist

Dr. Peter Onu was a Nigerian diplomat and philanthropist. He served as an ambassador to Ethiopia. He also served as the political officer of the Nigerian embassy in Moscow.[1] He then served as the acting secretary-general of the Organisation of African Unity between 1983 and 1985. He succeeded Edem Kodjo OAU. He was educated at the University of Nigeria, Nsuka. He built a law library in Lagos State, Nigeria. Until his death, he served as an adviser to a few of Nigeria's military heads of state.

REMEMBERING

AMBASSADOR PETER ONU'S LEGACY

1976

Dr. Onu expresses the need for support in the struggle against apartheid and injustice, he said, should not be left to the freedom fighters and the O.A.U.

1976

Ambassador Peter Onu, who was the spokesman for Organisation of African Unity (OAU) calls on the U.N. to give material aid to the Africans fighting the Apartheid.

1977

Assistant secretary general of the Organisation of African Unity, Dr. Peter Onu greets the Ugandan President Amin at an OAU conference.

1979

Liberia: Ambassador Peter Onu defends Uganda at an OAU summit and calls out President Nyerere's deceit and treachery towards the people of Uganda.

1982

Ethiopia: Ambassador Peter Onu considers the African refugee crisis at a meeting. "The rate at which this number of refugees had increased since the last chance or so is extremely great. In 1970, there were only 750,000. At present, the number of African refugees exceeds the population of many states on the continent. About one out of every 75 Africans is a refugee; and at the world level, one out of every two refugees is an African."

1985

Ethiopia: OAU secretary Ambassador Peter Onu warns of unprecedented catastrophe from drought and calls for heads of state and government to place the Economic problems in Africa at the forefront of deliberations.
